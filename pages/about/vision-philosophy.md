<h3>Our Vision</h3>
<p>To be a supportive, nurturing, inclusive educational service where children's wellbeing is promoted through natural play experiences. With families and the community, we strive to provide positive futures for our children.</p>
<h3>Philosophy</h3>
<p>At Gloucester Pre-School &amp; Early Years Learning Centre we view children as capable, competent co-contributors and active participants in their own learning. Early childhood education sets the foundation for positive lifelong learning.</p>
<p>Educators &amp; staff actively strive to provide an environment and atmosphere in which children and families feel emotionally safe, secure, &amp; a sense of belonging to our early years' education &amp; care community.</p>
<p>Learning occurs in a supportive unhurried natural environment where children's individual perspectives, interests, backgrounds, families and life experiences are celebrated. Education is provided through:</p>
<ul>
<li>Intentional teaching practices using children's interests.</li>
<li>Families' home cultural knowledge &amp; experiences.</li>
<li>Knowledge &amp; reconciliation with our local Aboriginal community, their beliefs &amp; culture.</li>
<li>Respect for our environment &amp; its resources.</li>
</ul>
<p>These will be used to promote ongoing inquiry, knowledge and confidence in children's individual capabilities.</p>
<p>Educational practices are influenced by theorists like Montessori, Dewey &amp; Gardner through the lens of Claire Warden's Nature Pedagogy. Our service promotes a natural learning environment that includes learning beyond the boundary fence, connecting us to our rural community. We promote children to assess and take risks and build resilience in a supportive environment. We understand the connection that caring for our environment supports the long-term health and wellbeing of current and future generations of children. Our culture of organisational growth is based on critical reflection and ongoing professional learning.</p>
