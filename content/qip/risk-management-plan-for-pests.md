<p>To manage pests in our service's indoor &amp; outdoor environments while understanding their role within a balanced natural environment.</p>
<p>To manage perceived pests while supporting minimal toxic chemical use and the health &amp; safety of staff, children &amp; visitors to our service. Connected to Sustainability Project Book pg 29, 10/3/2022</p>
<p>Outcomes</p>
<ul>
<li>Staff &amp; children are educated and supported in their knowledge to identify insects, arachnids (spiders), gastropods (slugs &amp; snails), hymenopters (wasps, bees &amp; ants), lepidoptera (moths, butterflies in all stages of development), reptiles, snakes, birds &amp; other rodents that may live or be present from time to time in our indoor &amp; outdoor environments.</li>
<li>Their life cycles, habits and risks to humans &amp; property are understood so controls and management can be practiced in an environmentally sustainable way.</li>
</ul>
<p>Short Term Strategies</p>
<ul>
<li>Identify what staff, children &amp; families consider are pests in our environment.</li>
<li>Understand their risks to the health &amp; wellbeing of the humans, resources, flora &amp; fauna.</li>
<li>Understand how to manage the risk using natural, low toxic or toxic chemicals.</li>
</ul>
<p>Long Term Strategies</p>
<ul>
<li>Design a resource to support the identification of pest risks in our indoor &amp; outdoor environments.</li>
<li>Design a risk assessment &amp; matrix to support management &amp; control measures of risks.</li>
<li>Ongoing support &amp; education in this area as new staff, committees &amp; families are involved with our organisation.</li>
</ul>
